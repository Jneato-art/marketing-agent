# Skill: Channel Strategy

## When to use
Whenever you must decide *where* to market. Most failed marketing is too many channels, too shallow.

## The decision framework

Score each candidate channel 1-5 on:
- **Audience fit** — is the buyer actually here?
- **Cost to play** — can the budget sustain it?
- **Speed to signal** — how fast will you learn if it works?
- **Compounding** — does effort build an asset (SEO, audience) or vanish (paid)?
- **Founder fit** — will the user actually keep doing it?

Pick the top 2-3. Run them properly before adding more.

## Channel cheat-sheet

| Channel | Best for | Speed | Compounds? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paid social (Meta/TikTok) | Fast testing, DTC | Fast | No |
| Search ads (Google) | High-intent demand | Fast | No |
| SEO / content | Durable inbound | Slow | Yes |
| Organic social | Brand + audience | Medium | Yes |
| Email / lifecycle | Retention, LTV | Fast | Yes |
| Partnerships / influencer | Borrowed trust | Medium | Sometimes |
| Community | Loyalty, word of mouth | Slow | Yes |

## Sequencing
Early stage: 1 fast channel (paid or outreach) to learn what converts + 1 compounding channel (SEO or email) to build an asset. Add channels only after the first two are working.

## Output
A ranked channel recommendation with scores, the chosen 2-3, and why — fed into `marketing-plan-builder`.

## Self-learning
Record realized CAC and conversion by channel in the index; let real numbers, not the cheat-sheet, drive the next decision.
